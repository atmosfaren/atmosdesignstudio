Parking Time Sweden

"How can Parking Time encourage motorists to switch from traditional license plates to their digital alternative?"

So what is a parking disc? Yes, that was a question we had to ask ourselves and why does it have so many challenges? A parking disc today is available handwritten, in paper form, plastic disk, a digital version and today in an application form in everyone's phones. So the question is how can we get the end users to use it in application? You will get answers to that in this project!

UX-CASE with the productowner

PARKING TIME PARKING SIGN

Background

"Parking Time Sweden, a startup with promising opportunities, faces challenges in a competitive market as they revolutionize parking spaces. Despite just a few years of experience, their innovative idea of digitizing the analog parking disc is gaining traction. Presently, their app is rudimentary, operating at a basic level with a limited MVP. Our challenge emerged from this context."

Scope

TOOLS: Figma, Microsoft, Google & Kanban

TIMELINE: 3 weeks & 2 iterations

TEAM: Productowner, UX Senior Supervisor & 4 UX Designers

Agile Methods, Teamworker, Design Process & Prototyping

UX-CASE with the productowner

THE PROBLEM

The Problem

"How can we help motorists use Parking Time when they have to park on a parking that needs a Parking disc?"

The project aimed to improve the parking experience for users by offering a digital parking service that replaces the traditional p-disk. We conducted research to understand user needs and challenges and developed a prototype and marketing plan to launch Parking Time.

We identified the need to simplify parking and increase accessibility for users by offering a digital solution. The research showed that the majority of users were open to trying an application instead of the physical p-disk.

Research Insights

  • 13/14 (93%) Uses paper disc
  • 9/14 (64%) Only knows about paper disc
  • 9/14 (64%) Prefer an app
  • 9/14 (64%) Looking for an app on the phone
  • 8/14 (57%) Checked for parking disc in the nearest store
  • 6/14 (43%) Writes a handwritten note
  • 5/14 (36%) Preferes a physical parking disc

Research Insights

Research Findings & Solutions

Research Findings & Solutions

User-Centric Design Solutions: By addressing specific pain points identified in user tests, such as simplifying the sign-in process, clarifying settings and icons, and improving the search function, the application becomes more intuitive and user-friendly.

Insights from User Testing: Understanding user preferences, behaviors, and challenges through user testing allowed for targeted improvements. For instance, incorporating options for quick sign-in with Google or registration numbers addresses user preferences and streamlines the onboarding process.

Implementation of Best Solutions: Incorporating features like bottom sheets for quick access, universal icons for intuitive understanding, and effective filters for parking zones improves the overall user experience. These solutions enhance accessibility, efficiency, and convenience for users navigating the application.

Marketing Strategy Alignment

The marketing plan is aligned with the identified objectives and target audience insights. Tailoring marketing efforts to diverse target groups and locations, establishing strategic partnerships, and engaging directly with potential users through events ensures effective promotion and adoption of the application.

Structured Implementation Process: Following a structured implementation process ensures efficiency and effectiveness in executing the marketing plan. From user testing to market launch, each step is strategically designed to maximize success and establish Parking Time as a prominent player in the parking app market.

Design Thinking Process

Design Thinking Process

FORMAT: Double Diamond, Analyzing methods, Formative Interviews, Personas, The 10 Heuristics, Different Matrix's, S.W.O.T., Affinity Diagrams, User Testing, Flow charts, Brainstorming & Site Maps.

SAMPLE SIZE: 14 Qualitative Interviews with people that uses parking discs and used open-ended questions for a deeper understanding. The users' ages were between 20-70 years old and lived around Sweden. They must have had used the parking disc at least once in some way.

TESTS: 7 User testings on different individuals that use a car or any transport with parking disc. This was crucial for us to know early if our first prototype had issues or unclear patterns in the design.

We did extensive research regarding parking discs & parking apps. We found only one competitor in a different region in Sweden. There is a huge market regarding this application and potential that we found within numerous big companies or states that use the parking disc.

Key Insights

SIMPLE & USER FRIENDLY: One big insight we had was to get the user to reach their choices within the functions with as few button presses as possible. This was a huge challenge but also the most satisfying one to manage.

KEY FEATURES: Early on, we noticed that the user wants to feel special, and the existing MVP had some problems but also missed the part of making the application more personal. The search function and the map became essential parts of the application.

BETTER INTERFACE: One important thing that made us take a big step regarding the interface was that the app stood out with its lime green color, but it was not a user-friendly color. So we made a huge change in color, font, and white spaces for more inclusiveness.

Key Insights

Conclusions

Conclusions

BOTTOM SHEET & FEATURES: With easy sign-in alternatives, the user can quickly move forward to the start page. Added nearest parking space, favorites & a variety of notifications. Different language was a no-brainer that will be more inclusive. The bottom sheet feature helped us a lot in making the application more user-friendly and adding more features with very few keystrokes.

MARKETING: For a small company, we noticed how important it is to have a secure model when implementing the application. We developed a secure strategic model that they can use when they find new partners or customers who want to use their application. Everything from slogans, starting points, marketing investments such as flyers or smaller sign material. What they can focus on in the beginning into the end cycle.

AGILE WORKFLOW: The key measures to find solutions for a quality product that we found were working with agile principles that we strictly adhered to during the whole process. There was always time for iterations and going back and forth between different stages during the design process. Always having standups, retros, using Kanban, and making sure we are on the right path but, of course, helping each other when needed.

Limitations

We reflected on the lessons learned from the project and identified key areas for improvement. This included the necessity for a more comprehensive breakdown of solutions and a deeper focus on certain aspects such as refining the onboarding process and incorporating additional features.

The project was executed by a diverse cross-functional team comprising designers, project managers, marketers, and product owners. We employed agile methodologies along with UX research and analysis, utilizing techniques such as formative interviews, prototypes, and user testing to develop and validate our solutions.

Upon reflection, we acknowledge certain regrets regarding our working approach. For instance, we realize that we disclosed individual information prematurely in the process. Additionally, we recognized belatedly that there were some group challenges that went unaddressed. During the presentation, we overlooked certain critical areas, such as onboarding, which prompted concerns from the product owner.

Limitations

Personal Take

Reflecting on the project, there are a few areas where I believe we could have improved:

  • Ensuring clearer anchoring within the solutions presented during the project's presentation.
  • Working towards creating a clearer picture of certain solutions to enhance understanding.
  • Avoiding premature division of work to allow for more flexibility and collaboration.
  • Requesting further iterations to refine and enhance our solutions.

In summary, through comprehensive research, prototyping, and testing, we successfully developed a user-centric digital parking service. However, future efforts will concentrate on enhancing the user experience and increasing awareness of Parking Time in the market.
